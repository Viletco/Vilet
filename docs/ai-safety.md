# AI safety

The server policy restricts Vilét-specific claims to a compact package assembled from published Services, Process, FAQ, About, Work evidence state, and Contact behavior. Internal files, authoring notes, secrets, legal comments, drafts, and unpublished projects are excluded.

Guardrails prohibit invented prices, timelines, proof, capabilities, guarantees, project acceptance, and delivery claims. They reject prompt-override and secret requests, discourage sensitive input, distinguish AI from ordinary automation, and require human review. Provider errors, timeouts, rate limits, unsafe requests, invalid output, and unexpected failures are normalized without raw identifiers or error bodies.

Logs must contain operational event categories only. They must not contain prompts, responses, summaries, URLs, inquiry content, raw IP addresses, credentials, or fetched HTML. No insights are collected because `AI_INSIGHTS_MODE=aggregate` is intentionally rejected until approved infrastructure exists.

Known limitations: policy checks are defense-in-depth rather than proof against every adversarial phrase; provider behavior requires preview evaluation; the current deterministic tools are preliminary discovery aids, not proposals or professional advice.
